Here are common situations to avoid that are common pitfalls

Don't tell people how to feel.
If you've ever had the urge to tell someone to "clam down", this is referring to that urge. One of the best pieces of advice I ever got when dealing with other people, and even more important with direct reports, is that people want to feel how they feel. A person who is upset will not respond well to being told to "calm down". No one responds well to being told they're invalid. This commonly comes off as the manager is projecting onto the direct report. This article does a good job of identifying the concept.
